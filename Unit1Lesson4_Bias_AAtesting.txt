
1.  You're testing advertising emails for a bathing suit company and you test one version of the email in February and the other in May.

    Sampling bias of the earlier February email as a different bathing suit type of a different demographic than the later email in May.  Different size one piece for the former and the latter is for smaller size two piece bathing suit could be a selection bias in the overall population of customers in the bathing suit company.


2. You open a clinic to treat anxiety and find that the people who visit show a higher rate of anxiety than the general population.

    Observer bias as the clinic workers push for anxiety treatment to visitors only.
    

3. You launch a new ad billboard based campaign and see an increase in website visits in the first week.

    Bias in assignment conditions as car drivers who pass the billboard remember the website address, while large groups of people who use public transportation like a subway that cannot view the billboard above ground.


4. You launch a loyalty program but see no change in visits in the first week.

    Context of the study, the loyalty program is launched during a holiday week when historically the number of customer visits are the lowest.
